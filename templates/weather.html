Understanding Park City Weather & Ski Conditions

Park City, Utah sits at 7,000 feet elevation with ski terrain rising to 10,000+ feet. The town averages 350-400 inches of snow per winter — one of the snowiest destinations in the lower 48 states. Our live conditions page pulls data directly from Deer Valley Resort, Park City Mountain Resort, UDOT road sensors, and 8 live webcams on the mountain, giving you a complete picture of current ski and driving conditions in one place.

Ski Season Calendar — When to Visit

Mid-November through early April is Park City ski season. Opening day typically falls around Thanksgiving when snowmaking has built a solid base. Early season (November-early December) features manmade snow on select trails. The best powder conditions arrive from mid-December through mid-March, with January and February offering the deepest natural snow base (typically 80-120 inches). Late March and April are "spring skiing" with softer afternoon snow, warmer temperatures (30s-50s°F), and fewer crowds. Resorts close in early-to-mid April, though late-season spring skiing can extend into May for Woodward Park City and some terrain parks.

Temperature & Weather Patterns

Winter temperatures at base elevation average 15-35°F during the day, dropping to 0-15°F overnight. Summit temperatures are typically 10-15°F colder due to altitude. Park City benefits from the "lake effect" — storms crossing the Great Salt Lake pick up moisture and drop it as dry powder snow on Park City (vs. heavier wet snow at coastal resorts). Summer temperatures range from 45°F overnight to 80-85°F daytime highs, with low humidity. Afternoon thunderstorms are common in July-August.

Driving Conditions: I-80, US-40, SR-224

The drive from Salt Lake City to Park City (I-80 East through Parley's Canyon) climbs from 4,300 ft to 7,020 ft at Parleys Summit in just 22 miles — a significant elevation gain that produces its own weather. Storms often affect Parley's Canyon more severely than downtown SLC or Park City proper. During major storms, UDOT implements traction laws (Level 1 requires M+S tires; Level 2 requires chains on 2WD or AWD with winter tires). All Rio Transportation vehicles are AWD SUVs with dedicated winter tires, meeting Level 2 requirements at all times. If I-80 closes due to weather or accidents, the alternate route is US-40 via Heber Valley (55-70 min from SLC, longer but less steep).

How to Use This Conditions Page

Check this page in the morning before a ski day: look at snow-stake cameras for fresh overnight accumulation, summit webcams for visibility (if socked in with fog, upper lifts may be slow or closed), base cameras for parking/crowd conditions, and road sensors for your drive. Our 24/48/72-hour snowfall totals help you decide if it's a powder day. The wind speed indicator matters — sustained winds above 30 mph often close upper-mountain lifts. For multi-day visits, check the forecast section to plan which days to ski and which to do non-ski activities (Main Street shopping, Utah Olympic Park tubing, snowshoeing at Round Valley).

Park City Weather & Conditions FAQ

When does Park City get the most snow?

January and February consistently receive the most snow — typically 80-120 inches per month combined. Early December through mid-March is prime powder season with frequent storm cycles.

How cold does it get in Park City during ski season?

Base elevation daytime: 15-35°F. Overnight: 0-15°F (occasionally -10°F during cold snaps). Summit temps are typically 10-15°F colder. Layer up with base layers, mid-layers, waterproof ski shell, hat, and gloves/mittens.

Does the mountain close for snow storms?

Rarely — ski resorts love snow storms and keep operating even during heavy snow. Upper lifts may close temporarily if winds exceed 40 mph. I-80 access to Park City can close briefly during severe storms for avalanche control or accidents.

Is Park City weather different from Salt Lake City?

Significantly different. Park City at 7,000 ft is 10-15°F colder than SLC at 4,300 ft and gets 2-3x more snow. SLC might be rainy/clear while Park City is in a blizzard. Always check Park City specifically — not downtown Salt Lake City forecasts.

When is Park City's summer season?

June through September. Summer activities include mountain biking (Park City Bike Park, Canyons Bike Park), alpine slide, hiking, concerts, farmer's market, and hot-air ballooning. Temperatures reach 80-85°F daytime with cool 45-55°F nights.
