Park City weather, explained.

Park City sits at 7,000 feet, with ski terrain rising to 10,000+. The town averages 350 to 400 inches of snow per winter — one of the snowiest destinations in the lower 48 states. This page pulls data directly from Deer Valley Resort, Park City Mountain Resort, UDOT road sensors and 8 live webcams.

Ski-season calendar.

Mid-November through early April is Park City ski season. Opening day typically falls around Thanksgiving when snowmaking has built a solid base. Best powder conditions arrive mid-December through mid-March, with January and February offering the deepest natural snow (typically 80-120 inches). Late March and April are "spring skiing" — softer afternoon snow, 30s-50s°F, fewer crowds. Resorts close in early-to-mid April; Woodward Park City and some terrain parks can run into May.

Temperature & weather patterns.

Winter temperatures at base elevation average 15-35°F during the day, dropping to 0-15°F overnight. Summit temps are typically 10-15°F colder. Park City benefits from the "lake effect" — storms crossing the Great Salt Lake pick up moisture and drop it as dry powder. Summer ranges from 45°F overnight to 80-85°F daytime, with low humidity; afternoon thunderstorms in July-August.

Driving conditions.

The drive from SLC to Park City (I-80 East through Parley's Canyon) climbs from 4,300 ft to 7,020 ft in just 22 miles — significant elevation that produces its own weather. Storms often affect Parley's more severely than downtown SLC or Park City. During major storms, UDOT implements traction laws: Level 1 requires M+S tires; Level 2 requires chains on 2WD or AWD with winter tires. All Rio Transportation vehicles are AWD SUVs with dedicated winter tires, meeting Level 2 at all times. Alternate route if I-80 closes: US-40 via Heber Valley (55-70 min from SLC, longer but less steep).

How to use this page.

Check this page in the morning before a ski day: snow-stake cameras for overnight accumulation, summit webcams for visibility, base cameras for parking and crowds, road sensors for the drive. Our 24/48/72-hour snowfall totals help you decide if it's a powder day. Wind speed matters — sustained winds above 30 mph often close upper-mountain lifts. For multi-day visits, use the forecast section to plan which days to ski and which to do non-ski activities (Main Street, Utah Olympic Park, snowshoeing at Round Valley).

Frequently asked.

When does Park City get the most snow?

January and February consistently receive the most — typically 80-120 inches per month combined. Early December through mid-March is prime powder season with frequent storm cycles.

How cold does it get during ski season?

Base elevation daytime: 15-35°F. Overnight: 0-15°F (occasionally -10°F during cold snaps). Summit temps are 10-15°F colder. Layer up: base, mid, waterproof shell, hat, gloves/mittens.

Does the mountain close for snow storms?

Rarely — ski resorts love snow and keep operating even during heavy storms. Upper lifts may close temporarily if winds exceed 40 mph. I-80 access can close briefly during severe storms for avalanche control or accidents.

Is Park City weather different from Salt Lake City?

Significantly different. Park City at 7,000 ft is 10-15°F colder than SLC at 4,300 ft, and gets 2-3x more snow. SLC might be rainy/clear while Park City is in a blizzard. Always check Park City specifically — not downtown SLC forecasts.

When is Park City's summer season?

June through September. Activities: mountain biking (Park City Bike Park, Canyons Bike Park), alpine slide, hiking, concerts, farmer's market, hot-air balloons. Temps reach 80-85°F daytime with 45-55°F nights.
